Our Story

From Kigali, to the World

Mavuta began in 2019 as a small atelier in Kigali, Rwanda — a studio where patterns were cut by hand and every stitch carried the intention of its maker. Founded by Ngabo Bruno, the brand emerged from a desire to create fashion that honored both craft and the modern woman.

The name Mavuta — drawn from the Kinyarwanda word for grace — became the north star. Not the flashy kind of grace, but the deeper kind. The kind you feel when a garment moves with you, speaks for you without saying a word.

Today, Mavuta designs are worn across East Africa and beyond. The studio still operates from its original space in Kigali, a light-filled room where new ideas begin the same way they always have — with fabric, scissors, and quiet focus.

What We Stand For

01

Craft First

Every garment is cut and finished in our Kigali atelier. We slow down so the work can be done right.

02

Material Honesty

We use natural fibres — linen, silk, cotton — sourced from ethical suppliers across the continent.

03

Quiet Identity

Our clothes carry no loud logos. The design is the signature. The fit is the statement.

04

Rooted in Africa

Our aesthetic is shaped by the landscapes, textures, and light of East Africa — not imported from elsewhere.
